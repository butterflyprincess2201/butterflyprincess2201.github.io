Make informed choices for housing or transportation in your new job location.

Welcome to Your Assignment

Congratulations on completing your education and landing a new job! This project will guide you through a real-life financial decision: evaluating housing or transportation costs in your new area.

Part One: Research

Step 1: Define Your Location & Job

  • Where is your new job? (City, suburb, town, or rural area?)
  • Research the average starting salary for your job in this location and estimate your monthly take-home pay.

Step 2: Choose Housing or Transportation

  • Housing: Options include renting, buying, or alternative housing like tiny homes.
  • Transportation: Options include leasing or buying a car, using public transport, carpooling, biking, or walking.

Step 3: Analyze Costs

  • Identify one-time and monthly expenses (e.g., rent, utilities, insurance, maintenance).
  • Consider how each option fits your budget and lifestyle.

Part Two: Compare Your Options

Use your research and calculations to write a 1-2 page essay. Cover these points:

  • A breakdown of costs for each option.
  • How these options fit your budget and lifestyle.
  • Your final choice and why, supported by your research.
